Arun Jayapal

Arun is a spiritual research professional, however currently working in the IT industry. He has completed PhD from the School of Computer science and Statistics, Trinity college Dublin. He worked on his thesis titled Finding diachronic sense changes by unsupervised methods under the guidance of Dr. Martin Emms during Sep 2013 and Apr 2018. For his complete list of publications, please look at his google scholar profile. He has also been a reviewer for the CoLing and EACL conferences.

Towards the end of his PhD tenure, he came back to India and started working with a start-up sayint based out of Hyderabad. He works as computational linguist on conversational analytics. The recorded contact-center conversations are converted to text by an ASR system and text analytics is carried out on those conversational text. He handles the complete NLP stack carried out for text analytics. Before his PhD, he worked earlier for Ditech networks -- now part of Nuance communications, on voice messaging platform. His role during that term was to de-normalize the ASR outcomes and convert them into human-readable form before delivering the text messages (converted from voice messages using ASR) to the users. Prior this he did his masters from the University of Sheffield, UK where he did his masters in speech and language processing, thesis titled Entity Identification & Co-reference Resolution guided by Dr. Rob Gaizauskas.

Off late I have not been very actively pushing my codebases to the github due to privacy reasons. However please do follow up on https://github.com/arunjeyapal to not miss any updates here.

  • Dynamic Generative model for Diachronic Sense Emergence Detection Proceedings of COLING 2016 by Martin Emms, Arun Jayapal held at Osaka, Japan in Dec. 2016 - A Gibbs sampling algorithm is developed for a diachronic model to estimate the parameters from raw time-stamped n-gram data with no sense annotation. We are able to demonstrate the inference of parameters which plausibly reflect the objective dynamics of sense use frequencies, in particular the emergence of a new sense.

  • An unsupervised EM method to infer time variation in sense probabilities Proceedings of ICON 2015: 12th International Conference on Natural Language Processing by Martin Emms, Arun Jayapal held at Trivandrum, India in Dec. 2015 - We developed an EM algorithm to estimate the parameters from raw time-stamped n-gram data with no sense annotation. We are able to demonstrate the inference of parameters which plausibly reflect the objective dynamics of sense use frequencies, in particular the emergence of a new sense.

  • Author Verification: Basic Stacked Generalization Applied To Predictions from a Set of Heterogeneous Learners by Erwan Moreau, Arun Jayapal, Gerard Lynch, Carl Vogel; PAN proceedings on Author verification, CLEF 2015 in Nov 2015 - Provides our system description for the author verification conducted by CLEF workshop.

  • Detecting change and emergence for multiword expressions by Martin Emms, Arun Jayapal; Proceedings of the 10th Workshop on Multiword Expressions (MWE 2014) held with EACL 2014 at Gotenburg, Sweden in Apr. 2014 - concerns unsupervised means to detect an emerging new 'MWE' usage of a given n-gram, such as the usage of smashed it to mean doing something excellently.

  • TCDSCSS: Dimensionality Reduction to Evaluate Texts of Varying Lengths-an IR Approach by Arun Jayapal, Martin Emms, John D. Kelleher; Proceedings of the 8th International Workshop on Semantic Evaluation (SemEval 2014), COLING held at Dublin, Ireland in Aug. 2014 - provides system description of the cross-level semantic similarity task for the SEMEVAL-2014 workshop, which uses dimensionality reduction technique.

  • Author Verification: Exploring a Large set of Parameters using a Genetic Algorithm by Erwan Moreau, Arun Jayapal, Carl Vogel; PAN proceedings on Author verification, CLEF 2015 in Sep. 2015 - concerns system description of Author verification task conducted by PAN for CLEF.

  • Vector space model and Overlap metric for Author Identification by Arun Jayapal, Binayak Goswami; PAN proceedings on Author verification, CLEF 2013 in Sep. 2013 - concerns system description of Author verification task conducted by PAN for CLEF. The system was developed using the very simple vector space model and worked reasonably well.

  • Similarity Overlap Metric and Greedy String Tiling for Plagiarism Detection by Arun Jayapal; PAN proceedings on Plagiarism detection, CLEF 2012 in Sep. 2012 - concerns system description of Plagiarism detection task conducted by PAN for CLEF. The system was judged first in the document retrieval task (one of two tasks involved in Plagiarism detection system).

  • USFD at KBP 2011: Entity linking, slot filling and temporal bounding by Amev Burman, Arun Jayapal, Sathish Kannan, Madhu Kavilikatta, Ayman Alhelbawy, Leon Derczynski, Robert Gaizauskas; KBP 2011 proceedings on Knowledge base Population for Text Analysis Conference in Jan. 2011 - concerns system description for the Question answering task and this was part of my Master's dissertation. My role was to come up the Named entitity recognition (NER) model and co-reference resolution model. For NER, I used stanford CRF classifier and for co-reference resolution, I had come-up with a rule-based classifier.
